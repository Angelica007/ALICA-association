African Leaders International Convergent Association

Our association is a nonprofit organisation operating in Africa, it deals with the transformation of young leaders to good future leaders of the tomorrow Africa.

We support leadership to thrive in contemporary cultures We partner with organisations seeking to develop responsible and sustainable organisations. We support them on their journey to thrive within contemporary cultures.

  • We bring together young leaders in Africa.




  • We form teams to discuss about challenges faced on our continents.



  • We train and transform those young leaders to future leaders with a growth mindest, commitment and integrity.


Our approach

ALICA aims to develop 3 million ethical and entrepreneurial leaders for Africa and the world by 2035. It uses a personalized, student-driven, project-based, and mission-oriented approach to create agile, lifelong leaders who can adapt to a changing world. ALICA's first site was inaugurated in September 2015 in Mauritius. It now has sites in Rwanda and Kenya, and is rolling out several more over the next few years.

Our leadership

Our Chancellor

Graça Machel

Graça Machel, an accomplished Mozambican politician, international advocate for women and children’s rights serves as the Chancellor of ALICA. Machel was the Minister of Education in Mozambique and led a movement to cut the illiteracy rate by 22 percent, and to increase the school-going population from 400,000 to 1.6 million. Machel was awarded the InterAction Humanitarian award in 1997 and was made an honorary Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire at the request of British Prime Minister Gordon Brown in 2007. She has also founded various organisations that work to ensure the rights of families and communities across Africa. These include the Graça Machel Trust, an organisation which protects women and children’s rights and promotes women’s leadership across Africa. The Trust also runs the Graça Machel Scholarships for Women. She is a Founding Member of the Elders, an independent group of global leaders who work together for peace and human rights.

Geoffrey Rugege, PhD

Our Vice-Chancellor

Dr. Rugege is an esteemed higher education leader and a renowned linguistics expert who has played a pivotal role in the lexicographical development of the Kinyarwanda language. He is the former Executive Director of the National Council for Higher Education Higher Education Council in Rwanda and is a prominent consultant in Higher Education within East, Central and Southern Africa. Born in Uganda, Dr. Rugege was a professor of English and linguistics at Grambling State University (Grambling, Louisiana) and Fayetteville State University (Fayetteville, North Carolina) for 28 years before retiring as a teacher. He holds a Bachelor’s degree in English from Makerere University, Kampala, Uganda and an M.A. and PhD in linguistics from the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. Dr. Rugege has edited two anthologies of African-American literature: The Black Orpheus published by Simon & Schuster (1996) and Introduction to Literature: Drama, Poetry and Prose by Thomson Publishers (2002) and his lexicology work has led to the publishing of the Kinyarwanda-English Dictionary (2010 Fountain Publishers, Kampala) and significant contributions to the Kamusi Project, Yale University’s online interactive dictionary of African languages. In addition to serving as ALU’s vice-chancellor, Dr. of Rugege is also a board member of Governors of the University Rwanda, Chancellor of the Kigali Institute of Management, and a member of the Rwanda Academy of Language and Culture.

Donald Kaberuka

Chairman of our Global Advisory Council

Kaberuka is a Rwandan economist and was the president of the African Development Bank from September 2005 until September 2015. He studied at the University of Dar es Salaam as an undergraduate and obtained his MPhil in Development Studies from University of East Anglia in 1979. He received his PhD in economics from the University of Glasgow. Prior to serving as President of the African Development Bank, Kaberuka served as Minister of Finance and Economic Planning in Rwanda for 8 years. He is credited with helping to stabilize the Rwandan economy from the effects of the 1994 genocide.


The Global Advisory Council consists of world-renowned experts in academia and the private sector who advise ALICA and its management team on key long-term strategic decisions and serve as global ambassadors for the institution. The Council’s members bring tremendous wisdom and experience to ALICA.

A network to All People

Leadership skills alone are not enough to make maximum impact. Effective leaders require a powerful network of peers, investors, mentors, and more to achieve their full potential. ALICA's people become part of a talented, diverse community from around the world. They are surrounded by driven, talented, and passionate people. They are exposed to ALICA’s unique and powerful network of mentors, guest speakers, experts-in-residence, industry leaders, investors, ALICA staff, and employers. They participate in events like the African Leadership Network and are provided opportunities to attend conferences where they can meet a broader set of influential people.
